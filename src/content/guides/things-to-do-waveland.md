Waveland is the beach town of Hancock County. Sitting directly on the Gulf of Mexico with miles of sandy shoreline, it draws visitors and residents who come for the water, the open air, and the easygoing pace of life. While Waveland is smaller and quieter than some of its Gulf Coast neighbors, that simplicity is a big part of its appeal. This is a place built around outdoor living, and there is plenty to do if you know where to look.

## The Beach

The defining feature of Waveland is its beach. The long stretch of white sand along Beach Boulevard is open to the public and provides one of the most accessible Gulf shorelines in Mississippi. Swimming, sunbathing, wading, and beachcombing are the main draws, and the relatively calm Gulf waters make it a good option for families with young children. The beach is rarely overcrowded, even during peak summer months, which means you can usually find a quiet stretch of sand without much effort.

Sunrise and sunset views from the Waveland beach are exceptional, with unobstructed sightlines across the Gulf. Many locals make a habit of evening walks along the shore as the sun goes down.

## Buccaneer State Park

Just east of downtown Waveland, Buccaneer State Park is one of the best outdoor recreation spots in Hancock County. The park sits on the Gulf shoreline and offers camping, picnic areas, a disc golf course, nature trails, and a wave pool that is popular with families during the summer. The campground provides both RV hookups and tent sites, many of them shaded by old-growth live oaks draped with Spanish moss.

Buccaneer State Park is also a good base for exploring the broader area. Its location on the beach makes it easy to combine a day of hiking or disc golf with an afternoon of swimming.

## Fishing and Water Sports

Waveland's position on the Gulf makes it a natural starting point for fishing. Pier fishing, surf fishing, and charter boat trips are all available in the area. Local anglers target speckled trout, redfish, flounder, and sheepshead depending on the season. Kayaking and paddleboarding are also popular in the calmer waters near the shore and in the bayous and marshes that run inland from the coast.

## Gardendale Playground and Community Spaces

For families, Waveland maintains several parks and playgrounds throughout the community. Gardendale Playground and other neighborhood green spaces provide places for children to play and for families to gather. The city also hosts community events throughout the year, including holiday celebrations and outdoor festivals that bring the town together.

## Exploring Beyond Waveland

Waveland's location makes it easy to combine a beach day with a visit to neighboring Bay St. Louis, which is just minutes away and offers galleries, restaurants, and shopping. The rural areas north of Waveland, including Kiln and the Jourdan River corridor, provide additional opportunities for nature exploration, fishing, and experiencing the quieter side of Hancock County.

## A Beach Town at Its Core

Waveland does not try to be something it is not. It is a beach town, and it does that well. If you are looking for Gulf Coast relaxation, outdoor activity, and a community that takes life at a comfortable pace, Waveland is the place to be.
