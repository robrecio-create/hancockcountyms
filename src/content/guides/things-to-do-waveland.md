Waveland is Hancock County's Gulf-front town. It doesn't get the tourist traffic of Biloxi or the artsy reputation of Bay St. Louis, and for the most part that's fine by the people who live there.

## Buccaneer State Park

This is the main draw. Buccaneer State Park covers 396 acres along the beachfront and has:

- A **wave pool** open Memorial Day through Labor Day — genuinely popular with families
- Camping sites with hookups and primitive options
- Beach access
- A spray park for younger kids
- Walking and biking trails

Admission for the wave pool is modest. The camping reservations fill up on summer weekends, so book early.

## The Beach

Waveland has beach access along the Gulf. Quieter than the Biloxi/Gulfport stretch. The sand and water are roughly the same — the difference is just the crowd level.

## Casino Magic Site

The original Casino Magic site was never rebuilt after Katrina. The vacant land along the beachfront is a reminder of how Waveland looked before the storm. The town has rebuilt significantly, but some scars remain.

## Dining

Waveland has a small but solid selection of local restaurants. Seafood is the anchor. A handful of places have been there long enough to have regulars who come weekly. Ask locals where they actually eat.

## Getting There

Waveland is on Highway 90, the beach road that runs the entire length of the Mississippi Gulf Coast. Bay St. Louis is five miles east. Slidell, Louisiana is about thirty miles west.
