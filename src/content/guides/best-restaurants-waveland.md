Waveland sits right on the Gulf of Mexico shoreline, and its restaurant scene reflects that beachside identity. This small coastal city may not have the sprawling dining district of its neighbor Bay St. Louis, but it offers honest, satisfying food with a character all its own. From seafood shacks near the beach to neighborhood favorites tucked along Highway 90, Waveland rewards those who know where to look.

## Beach Boulevard and Highway 90

Much of Waveland's dining is concentrated along Beach Boulevard and the Highway 90 corridor. These restaurants serve the locals who live here year-round as well as the visitors who come for the white sand beaches and laid-back Gulf Coast atmosphere. You will find casual spots specializing in fried seafood platters, po'boys, and plate lunches that change with the day of the week. The vibe is unhurried and unpretentious, which is exactly what most people are looking for when they sit down to eat near the water.

## Gulf Seafood Done Right

Waveland's location on the Gulf means fresh seafood is a given. Local restaurants serve shrimp, oysters, crab claws, and whatever the day's catch brings in. Many places keep their menus simple, focusing on quality ingredients prepared without unnecessary fuss. A fried shrimp po'boy on French bread, dressed with lettuce, tomato, and a smear of remoulade, is as close to a perfect lunch as you will find on the Mississippi coast.

Boiled seafood is also a staple here, especially during crawfish season in the spring and early summer. Several spots offer boiled crawfish, shrimp, and crab by the pound, seasoned with the spicy Cajun blends that define Gulf Coast cooking.

## Neighborhood Gems

Beyond the main corridors, Waveland has a handful of neighborhood restaurants that are worth seeking out. These are the kind of places where the staff knows most customers by name and the menu reflects what the owner likes to cook. Southern comfort food, including fried chicken, smothered pork chops, and homemade sides like butter beans and cornbread, is well represented. Breakfast spots serve biscuits and gravy alongside eggs and grits, fueling early mornings before a day at the beach.

## A Resilient Food Scene

Waveland was hit hard by Hurricane Katrina in 2005, and the city's restaurant landscape has been rebuilt with the same determination that defines the community. Many of the restaurants here were started or restarted by locals who chose to stay and rebuild. That resilience shows up in the food and in the welcome you receive when you walk through the door.

Whether you are grabbing a quick bite after a morning on the beach or sitting down for a full seafood dinner, Waveland's restaurants deliver the kind of straightforward, satisfying meals that keep people coming back to the Gulf Coast.
