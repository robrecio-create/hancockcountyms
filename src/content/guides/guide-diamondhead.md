Diamondhead is not like the rest of Hancock County. It's a master-planned community built in the 1970s, complete with golf courses, a lake, and a distinct suburban character. It's also now an incorporated city, which surprised some people who'd been calling it a "community" for decades.

## The Golf

Diamondhead Corporation built the community around two golf courses, and they're still the main attraction for many residents and visitors.

**Pine** and **Cardinal** courses wind through the residential development. Both are 18-hole layouts. Tee times are available to the public. The clubhouse has a restaurant and bar that's popular with residents.

## The Lake

Diamondhead's man-made lake is a centerpiece of the community. Residents fish from their backyards, kayak, and paddle around. Not open to the public as an attraction per se, but a nice feature of the area.

## Shopping and Dining

A small commercial strip serves the residential community. The usual necessities — pharmacy, grocery, a handful of restaurants. Nothing that would drive you from Gulfport or Bay St. Louis, but enough that residents don't have to leave for basics.

## Location

Diamondhead is on I-10 in northern Hancock County, positioned between Bay St. Louis (south) and the Louisiana border (west). It's a convenient location if you're driving the coast and need to stop for fuel, food, or a golf round.

## The Community

About 8,000 people call Diamondhead home. The architecture is uniformly suburban — single-family homes, well-maintained yards, quiet streets. Very different vibe from the bayou character of Kiln or the artsy charm of Bay St. Louis.
