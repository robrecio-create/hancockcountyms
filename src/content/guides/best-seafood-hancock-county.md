Hancock County's seafood scene is underrated. The county doesn't have the casino hotel restaurants or the tourist volume of Harrison County, which means the places that exist here are primarily serving locals. That's usually a good sign.

## What's in Season

The same Gulf species that show up in Biloxi and Gulfport are available here:

**Gulf shrimp** — Brown shrimp season runs roughly May through December. White shrimp are available year-round but peak in fall. Buy from a local seafood market if you want to cook your own; restaurants that source locally will tell you.

**Oysters** — Mississippi Sound oysters are smaller and saltier than Pacific varieties. Best October through April. Several spots in Bay St. Louis serve them raw or chargrilled.

**Blue crab** — Summer through early fall. Soft shell crabs in season (briefly) are excellent if you find them.

**Redfish** — The local name for red drum. "On the half shell" (skin-on, grilled) is the preparation you want.

## Where to Look

Bay St. Louis has the most restaurant options in the county. The old town has several spots that do seafood well — not fine dining necessarily, but honest preparation with fresh ingredients.

The waterfront spots have views of St. Louis Bay. Ask which ones actually source locally rather than using frozen product.

**Waveland** has a few local seafood spots that tend to be more casual and less expensive than their Bay St. Louis counterparts.

## Seafood Markets

If you're cooking, find a local seafood market and buy directly. Hancock County has a few — look along the bay road and in the commercial areas near the water. Fresh-caught Gulf shrimp from a local vendor beats anything you'll find in a grocery store freezer.

## Red Flags

- "Gulf shrimp" that's suspiciously cheap (often Vietnamese import)
- Large, uniformly sized shrimp (wild-caught Gulf shrimp vary in size)
- Menus with no mention of sourcing
