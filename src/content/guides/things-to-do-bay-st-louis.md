Bay St. Louis is the kind of place people visit once and immediately start thinking about moving to. It's small, walkable, has a genuine art scene, and sits right on the water.

## Old Town

The historic district downtown is the reason most people make the trip. A compact grid of streets with art galleries, boutiques, restaurants, and bars in old buildings. It's walkable in a way that very few Mississippi towns are.

**Main Street** is the spine. Walk it slowly. The side streets have good things too.

The galleries are real — not tourist-trap souvenir art but actual working artists with studios and gallery spaces. Several are open on weekends.

## The Beach

Bay St. Louis has its own beach along the bay (St. Louis Bay, which opens into the Gulf). Calmer water than the open Gulf. Good for kayaking, paddleboarding, and watching the sunset over the water.

**Beach Boulevard** runs along the bay with views across to Pass Christian. The drive along the water at sunset is excellent.

## Bay Town Inn and the Historic District

The antebellum homes along the beach road and in the historic district are remarkable survivors of Katrina. Many were lost; many were rebuilt. The ones that remain — or were faithfully reconstructed — give a sense of what the Mississippi Gulf Coast looked like before development.

## The Arts

Bay St. Louis has a monthly art walk. Galleries open late, wine flows, you walk from place to place. Check dates before your trip — it happens on a specific Saturday each month.

The **BSL Arts District** is concentrated in the old town. The annual Wizard Dunes festival each fall is a big draw.

## Water Activities

- **Kayaking** — Launch from several points along the bay
- **Fishing** — The bay is shallow and productive; redfish, trout, flounder
- **Sailing** — The Bay St. Louis Yacht Club hosts races

## Day Trips

- **New Orleans**: 50 miles west, about an hour
- **Waveland**: 10 minutes east on the coast road
- **Pass Christian**: 20 minutes east
